▸ Should IPO proceeds go toward bank loans? BSEC faces growing demand
THE FINANCIAL EXPRESS
Issuer companies and BAPLC are pressing BSEC to raise or scrap the 30% cap on using IPO/RPO proceeds to repay bank loans; brokers and ICB's Abu Ahmed say it should hinge on whether the debt funded genuine expansion, cautioning against enabling banks to simply clean up books without helping market growth.
■ 04 / MONEY MARKET & POLICY
 
▸ NBR intensifies nationwide monitoring of withholding tax compliance
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
NBR has deployed special tax-zone teams to verify withholding-tax compliance nationwide, reminding businesses of officials' Section 147 powers to inspect premises, records and digital devices (including bypassing passwords), with penalties for obstruction under Section 147(2).
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ BSEC turns to digital authorisation, AI surveillance to curb broker fraud
THE FINANCIAL EXPRESS
BSEC Chairman Masud Khan announced a fully digital investor-authorisation system via a new DSE back-office platform to stop unauthorised account changes, plus a one-year mandate to convert DSE surveillance into an AI-based system that auto-detects abnormal price moves and can trigger automatic trading suspensions.
▸ Sustainable finance key to Bangladesh's low-carbon transition: BSEC chief
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
BSEC Chairman Masud Khan told the Sustainability Summit 2026 that green, social, sustainability and blue bonds are becoming key global financing instruments Bangladesh must tap, stressing stronger governance and credible ESG disclosure to build investor confidence and avoid greenwashing.

▸ Local airlines go for biggest-ever billion dollar expansion ahead of new 3rd Terminal opening
THE BUSINESS STANDARD TRAVEL
Biman, US-Bangla, Air Astra and Novoair plan to grow their combined fleet 78.4% to 91 aircraft by 2027 ahead of HSIA's Third Terminal, led by US-Bangla's $1.11bn, 21-aircraft order; foreign carriers still hold ~65% of Bangladesh's international passenger market.
