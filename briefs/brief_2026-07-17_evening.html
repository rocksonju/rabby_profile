■ 01 / CAPITAL MARKET
 
▸ DSEX snaps rally as profit-taking drags, turnover falls 26%
BSS
DSEX fell 25.9 points (−0.44%) to close at 5,900 on 16 July, snapping a five-session rally, as profit-taking in major stocks weighed on the market; turnover declined 26.2% to Tk1,120cr from Tk1,520cr, with pharma (17.4%), textiles (13.2%) and general insurance (10.6%) leading turnover.
■ 02 / COMPANIES
 
▸ GP logs lowest H1 profit in at least 4 years despite 105% interim dividend
THE DAILY STAR  TELECOM
Grameenphone's H1 2026 revenue fell 2.49% to Tk7,739cr and profit dropped 6% to Tk1,420cr YoY, with Q2 net profit down 14% to Tk758cr; the board still declared a 105% interim cash dividend, citing continued cost discipline and subscriber growth to 8.63 crore.
▸ BSRM plans Tk10,000cr flat steel plant to cut import dependence
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  ENGINEERING
BSRM plans a Tk10,000cr integrated hot-rolled flat steel plant at Mirsarai Economic Zone, Bangladesh's first, with 15-lakh-tonne annual capacity that could replace up to Tk15,000cr in imports; the company has sought a 10-year 10% supplementary duty on imports to make the project bankable.
■ 03 / MACRO ECONOMY
 
▸ Invest Bangladesh Bill passed, four agencies to merge
THE DAILY STAR
Parliament passed the Invest Bangladesh Bill 2026 by voice vote, merging Bida, Beza, BHTPA and the PPP Authority into a single Invest Bangladesh Authority to simplify investor services and cut bureaucratic delays; opposition MPs objected to its urgent passage.
▸ Bepza secures record $718m investment proposals in FY26, two-thirds from Chinese firms
THE DAILY STAR
Bepza signed lease agreements worth a record $718m with 36 companies in FY26, up from $498m in FY25; 23 of the 36 are Chinese-owned or joint ventures worth $499m, and the projects are expected to create 75,744 jobs once operational.
▸ BEPZA exports grow 2.2% to $8.41bn in FY26, now 17.51% of national exports
BSS
Exports from Bepza-run zones rose 2.2% to $8.41bn in FY26 even as Bangladesh's overall exports fell 0.58% to $48bn, lifting Bepza's share of national exports to 17.51% from 17.03% a year earlier.
▸ Exports through Benapole halve in FY26 as trade curbs deepen crisis
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
Exports through Benapole land port to India fell to 189,358 tonnes in FY26 from 381,440 tonnes a year earlier as reciprocal India-Bangladesh trade restrictions since August 2024 curbed jute, RMG, chemicals and other categories, hitting C&F agents and port revenue.
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ BB returns Al-Arafah Islami Bank to former owners
THE DAILY STAR  BANK
Bangladesh Bank has returned Al-Arafah Islami Bank PLC to its founding sponsor-shareholders, reconstituting the board with 14 returning directors including KDS Group's Khalilur Rahman, after the bank's post-2024 board improved its financial condition; the bank remains in DSE's Z category after two years without a cash dividend.
▸ Reports of suspicious transactions rose 74% in FY25
THE DAILY STAR
BFIU received 30,199 suspicious transaction/activity reports in FY25, up 74% YoY and the highest since FY21, with banks filing about 90% of reports; the agency said Tk76,000cr in assets have been frozen across 11 priority cases, including the Sheikh family.

THE BUSINESS STANDARD  REALESTATE
Bangladesh's housing sector continues to struggle with falling sales and rising construction costs, squeezing developers and homebuyers alike.
▸ Rupsha 800MW power plant seeks one-year extension as long-term gas supply remains uncertain
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  FUELPOWER
NWPGCL has proposed cutting the Rupsha 800MW plant's cost by Tk1,736cr (about 20%) while extending completion to June 2027; Petrobangla has only assured gas for the 107-day commissioning phase, with long-term commercial gas supply still undecided.
