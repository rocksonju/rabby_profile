■ DSE UPDATE
 
Dhaka's bourses are closed today (Friday) for the weekend. In the last session (Thursday, 16 July), DSEX fell 25.92 points, or 0.44%, to close at 5,900.36, snapping a five-session rally as profit-taking in recently gained stocks dominated trade; DS30 slipped 15.28 points (-0.68%) to 2,227.63. Turnover fell 26.2% to BDT 1,118cr from BDT 1,516cr the prior session, and breadth was negative — 103 advanced, 240 declined, 52 unchanged of 395 traded. Pharmaceuticals led turnover (17.4%), followed by textiles (13.2%) and general insurance (10.6%); life insurance (-1.7%), general insurance (-1.4%) and cement (-1.3%) led sector decliners while tannery (+1.2%), travel & leisure (+0.6%) and IT (+0.5%) advanced. YTD the DSEX has returned +21.27% (base: 4,865.34 on 30 Dec 2025).

THE BUSINESS STANDARD  ENGINEERING
BSRM plans a Tk10,000cr integrated hot-rolled flat steel plant in Mirsarai Economic Zone that could replace Tk14,000-15,000cr of annual imports; seeks 10-year supplementary duty protection, sparking monopoly-risk concerns at the finance ministry.
■ 03 / MACRO ECONOMY
 
▸ IMF cuts Bangladesh GDP growth forecast to 3.5pct for FY27
XINHUA
IMF mission chief Ivo Krznar said growth will slow to 3.5% in FY27 (from an earlier 4.3% estimate) and below 3% over the medium term absent reforms; contrasts with World Bank (4.6%) and ADB (4.5%) forecasts.
▸ Exports through Benapole halve in FY26 as trade curbs deepen crisis
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
Exports via Benapole land port fell to 189,358 tonnes in FY26 from 381,440 tonnes in FY25 as reciprocal India-Bangladesh trade restrictions since Aug 2024 disrupted cross-border commerce.
■ 04 / MONEY MARKET & POLICY
 
▸ Treasury bill, bond yields fall amid high investments
NEW AGE
91-day T-bill yield dropped to 9.30% by 6 July from 10.42% in early June as government bank borrowing eased post-FY26 close and surplus banking-system liquidity reduced pressure on government securities.
■ 06 / SECTOR NEWS
 
▸ Bangladesh loses EU apparel market share faster than rivals
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  TEXTILE
Eurostat data show Bangladesh EU apparel exports fell 18.89% in Jan-May 2026 vs a 9.96% overall EU import decline, cutting its market share to 21.5% from 23.9%, underperforming China, Vietnam, India and Turkey ahead of LDC graduation.
▸ Housing sector sinks deeper amid plunging sales, soaring costs
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  REAL ESTATE
Apartment registrations fell 19.6% in H1 2026 vs the 2025 average as soaring material costs and a new 15% capital-gains tax on apartment registrations under the FY27 budget deepen a multi-year housing slump.
