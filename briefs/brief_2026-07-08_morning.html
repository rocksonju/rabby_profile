■ DSE UPDATE
 
DSEX closed at 5,770, retreating 0.19% for the day (−11 points) as profit-taking extended the benchmark's correction into a second straight session, with selective buying in low-cap and momentum names limiting the decline. Turnover fell 16.7% to roughly BDT 1,160cr from BDT 1,380cr in the previous session. Textiles led turnover (21.0% share), followed by general insurance (9.6%) and engineering (8.8%); of 396 traded issues, 145 advanced, 187 declined and 64 were unchanged. The CSE also closed lower (CSCX −33.3, CASPI −47.2). YTD, the DSEX has returned +18.59% (base: 4,865.34 on 30 Dec 2025).

▸ Govt committed to restoring investors' confidence in capital market, PM tells JS
BSS
PM Tarique Rahman told parliament the government remains committed to restoring capital market stability, citing 17 reform priorities including BSEC leadership changes, floor-price withdrawal, an FPI onboarding portal, and Tk1,497cr in fines already imposed for market manipulation.
■ 03 / MACRO ECONOMY
 
▸ Bangladesh FDI rises 45% but still trails major regional peers
THE DAILY STAR
UNCTAD's World Investment Report 2026 shows Bangladesh FDI rose 45% to $1.78bn in 2025, ending two years of decline, but the country remained third in South Asia behind India ($38.9bn) and Pakistan ($1.85bn).
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ Depositors of troubled banks will get back money with interest: Khosru
BSS  BANK
Finance Minister Amir Khosru Mahmud Chowdhury assured parliament there will be no "haircut" for depositors of five distressed banks merged into Sammilito Islami Bank, though repayment will take time as the banks are loss-making; deposit insurance ceiling raised to Tk200,000.
