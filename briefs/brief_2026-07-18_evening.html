■ 01 / CAPITAL MARKET
 
▸ Entrepreneurs seek to use more than 70% of IPO proceeds for debt repayment
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
At a BSEC stakeholder consultation on the Public Offer of Equity Securities Rules, 2025, listed companies' representatives proposed raising the cap on using IPO/RPO proceeds for long-term debt repayment from 30% to over 70%; merchant bankers separately proposed removing the Tk125cr paid-up capital cap for fixed-price IPOs.
■ 03 / MACRO ECONOMY
 
▸ Bangladesh's gross forex reserves rise to $36.66bn
BSS
Bangladesh Bank data showed gross forex reserves at $36.66bn as of 16 July, while reserves under the IMF's BPM6 methodology stood at $31.97bn.
▸ IMF acknowledges Bangladesh's reform efforts, to continue talks on new support programme
BSS
An IMF staff mission that visited Dhaka 12-16 July called talks with authorities constructive; discussions on a new Fund-supported programme's size and reform commitments continue in coming months, with the Fund citing Middle East-driven cost pressures and urging revenue mobilisation, subsidy rationalisation and banking-sector reform.
▸ Apparel exports to EU, US tumble in Jan-May
THE DAILY STAR  TEXTILE
Apparel exports to the EU fell 18.89% YoY to €7.28bn and to the US fell 8.08% to $3.25bn in Jan-May, underperforming most competitors; Bangladesh's global apparel market share slipped to 6.76% in 2025, with industry voices flagging LDC-graduation tariff risk absent GSP+ benefits.
▸ Muktadir seeks 3 years extension to LDC graduation preparatory period
BSS
Commerce Minister Khandakar Abdul Muktadir told ECOSOC's president and vice-president at UN Headquarters that Bangladesh needs a three-year extension to its LDC-graduation preparatory period to consolidate reforms and strengthen the financial sector amid the country's economic-political transition and global uncertainty.
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ BSEC clarifies proposed Margin Rules amendment, cautions against misleading reports
BSS
BSEC said the draft Margin Rules, 2025 amendment was only approved in principle at its 913th meeting and is not final, warning that some pre-release media reports were incomplete and misleading; the draft will be published for public comment before final approval.
■ 06 / SECTOR NEWS
 
▸ Business Plus / Pharma industry's next big leap
THE DAILY STAR  PHARMACEUTICALS
Bangladesh's pharma industry, meeting ~98% of domestic demand and exporting to ~166 countries for over $200m annually, is pushing into biologics and complex generics -- Beximco Pharma launched a generic cystic-fibrosis drug at 96% below the innovator price and Eskayef became the first BD firm to export complex injectables to the US.
▸ Huarun Tex to invest $30m in BEPZA Economic Zone
BSS  TEXTILE
Chinese firm Huarun Tex signed a land-lease deal with BEPZA on 16 July to build a $30m yarn and woven-fabric plant at the Mirsharai Economic Zone, targeting 24,000 tonnes of yarn and 20 million metres of grey fabric annually and creating about 580 jobs.
