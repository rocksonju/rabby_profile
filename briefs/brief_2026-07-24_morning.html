■ 02 / COMPANIES
 
▸ Southeast Bank approves 10% dividend for 2025
THE DAILY STAR  BANK
Southeast Bank's 9th EGM and 31st AGM approved a 10% dividend (3% cash, 7% stock) for 2025; 2025 consolidated operating profit was Tk1,197.84cr, EPS Tk2.51, NAV per share Tk25.74. Shareholders also approved raising authorised capital from Tk1,500cr to Tk2,000cr.
▸ Singer returns to profit in Q2 2026
THE DAILY STAR  ENGINEERING
Singer Bangladesh swung to a Tk14.1cr pre-tax profit in Q2 2026 versus a Tk27.9cr loss a year earlier, on revenue of Tk8.4bn (+3.4% YoY); EPS and operating cash flow per share improved, though NAV declined, as its BSEZ green factory ramps local manufacturing and exports.
■ 03 / MACRO ECONOMY
 
▸ Trade deficit widens 24% as exports fall and capital imports slump
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
Bangladesh's FY26 trade deficit (Jul-May) widened 23.7% YoY to $23.98bn as capital machinery imports fell 10.68% and exports missed the $55bn target at ~$48bn; a record $35.5bn in remittances cushioned the external account and produced a $4.16bn financial-account surplus.
▸ Bangladesh needs $15b annual FDI by 2030 to sustain growth: Ficci
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
Ficci's "FDI for a New Bangladesh" report says annual FDI must rise nearly sevenfold to $15bn by 2030; FDI/GDP is just 0.29-0.36% versus Vietnam's 4.23%, hurt by policy uncertainty, 23 agencies/150 services for approvals, and a 32.26% NPL ratio in banking.
▸ US readies new tariffs as Trump's 10% global levy to expire
THE DAILY STAR
Trump's temporary 10% global tariff expires this week; forced-labour-linked duties of 10-12.5% on roughly 60 partners are expected next. Bangladesh was among the countries previously named in USTR's forced-labour tariff review — a fresh risk to RMG export competitiveness.
■ 04 / MONEY MARKET & POLICY
 
▸ PRI proposes inclusive system to unify digital payments, cut remittance costs
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
PRI proposed a Mojaloop-based instant payment system to unify Bangladesh's closed-loop MFS ecosystem; only 43% of adults hold a financial account. Interoperable remittance corridors could save Bangladesh $250-400m/year versus the current ~6.5% global remittance cost.
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ Bangladesh ramps up anti-money laundering reforms before global review
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
Ahead of the APG's fourth mutual evaluation in 2027, Bangladesh is completing five national money-laundering/CFT risk assessments by December; an APG pre-evaluation team visits Dhaka Aug 11-13. A poor rating risks tighter LC and cross-border compliance costs for banks.
▸ BSEC, IMF discuss green bond market development
THE DAILY STAR  BOND
BSEC met an IMF Climate Policy technical assistance mission to discuss Bangladesh's green/sustainability bond taxonomy, verification and impact-reporting standards, and ways to channel capital-market funding into climate-resilient investment.
