■ 02 / COMPANIES
 
▸ Mutual Trust Bank approves 12% stock dividend at 27th AGM
DHAKA TRIBUNE  BANK
MTB's 27th AGM, held virtually on 16 July, approved a 12% stock dividend for the year ended 31 December 2025; MD & CEO Syed Mahbubur Rahman and the board, including Norfund-nominated director Daniel De Lange, attended.
■ 03 / MACRO ECONOMY
 
▸ Slow reforms may weigh on Bangladesh's growth: Fitch
THE DAILY STAR
Fitch warns delayed reforms could slow long-term growth and raise vulnerability to shocks, citing the government's review of NBR independence and Bank Resolution Ordinance measures; Fitch doubts the FY27 budget's 10.2% revenue-to-GDP target will be met.
▸ Skills mismatch demands labour policy reforms: experts
THE DAILY STAR
A CPD-organised webinar called for urgent labour-policy reforms — education-market alignment, lifelong reskilling, employment-linked industrial policy and social protection for gig workers — to manage digitalisation and persistent skills mismatches over the next decade.
▸ EU and G77 express support for Bangladesh's smooth, sustainable LDC graduation
BSS
At separate UN meetings, the EU delegation and the G77-and-China chair backed Bangladesh's LDC graduation as the commerce minister made the case for a three-year extension of the preparatory period; the EU also welcomed talks on a Bangladesh-EU FTA.
■ 04 / MONEY MARKET & POLICY
 
▸ Big firms look overseas as local credit costs too much
THE DAILY STAR
Large conglomerates including PRAN-RFL are turning to foreign lenders for cheaper financing as local borrowing costs stay high; Bangladesh Bank is preparing a framework to let private firms take long-term foreign loans up to a limit without prior approval, though economists warn of taka-depreciation risk.
▸ After banks, BB offers one-time exit to NBFI borrowers
THE DAILY STAR  NBFI
Bangladesh Bank extended a one-time loan-settlement facility to distressed NBFI borrowers; classified loans as of 30 June 2026 qualify if repaid in a lump sum, with priority for agriculture, CMSME and women's-entrepreneurship loans, as the sector's 35 NBFIs carry Tk29,408cr in bad loans (37.11% of disbursed loans).
▸ BB relaxes deposit cap for healthy NBFIs
THE DAILY STAR  NBFI
Bangladesh Bank will let financially sound finance companies accept deposits above the Tk50 lakh individual / Tk1 crore joint-account ceiling, provided they hold a classified-loan ratio of 10% or less and capital adequacy of at least 10%, effective immediately.
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ BB frames import rules for free trade zones
THE DAILY STAR
Bangladesh Bank issued a framework letting authorised dealers and offshore banking units handle imports into the country's first free trade zones, restricted to manufacturers, authorised traders and logistics firms; consignment goods may sit 48-60 months and usance imports are capped at 270 days.
