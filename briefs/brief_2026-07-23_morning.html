■ 01 / CAPITAL MARKET
 
▸ Late-session sell-off drags DSEX as insurance margin row rattles investors
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
DSEX fell 27 points to 5,871 in a late-session sell-off as BSEC's proposed insurance margin-lending rule changes rattled investors; turnover still rose 7% to Tk1,211cr.
■ 02 / COMPANIES
 
▸ Jamuna Bank profit jumps 22% to Tk378cr in H1
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  BANK
Consolidated net profit rose 22% to Tk378.29cr in H1 2026 on higher investment income and lower loan-loss provisions; EPS rose to Tk4.03 from Tk3.31.
▸ Union Capital's H1 losses widen on lower interest income, loan recoveries
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  NBFI
Consolidated loss per share widened to Tk2.12 in H1 2026 from Tk1.60 a year earlier as NPL recoveries weakened; NAV per share fell deeper negative even as the stock rose 8.7% on the day.
▸ Samsung resumes Bangladesh production, plans local phone launch in January
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  ENGINEERING
Fair Electronics has restarted Samsung refrigerator production at its Narsingdi factory after an 18-month hiatus, with local smartphone assembly set to resume in January; the venture has drawn about Tk1,000cr in investment to date.
■ 04 / MONEY MARKET & POLICY
 
▸ NBR opens online income tax return filing for FY27, offers 5% early rebate
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
NBR's e-return portal for FY2026-27 individual tax filing is now live; filers between July and September get a 5% rebate on net tax payable (capped at Tk25,000), while late filers face additional tax.
▸ Ecnec approves 8 projects worth Tk14,041cr
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
Ecnec, chaired by PM Tarique Rahman, approved eight development projects worth Tk14,041cr (Tk10,494cr from state funds, Tk3,550cr via loans), spanning rural infrastructure, disaster shelters, the Dhaka-Ashulia expressway and power/gas exploration.
■ 05 / REGULATORY
 
▸ Tk9,367cr provision gap: BSEC orders Dhaka exchange to ensure lenders' quarterly reporting
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
BSEC has directed the DSE to enforce quarterly reporting of negative equity and unrealised losses after brokers and merchant banks left a Tk9,367cr provisioning shortfall as of May; non-compliant BO accounts face trading and dividend restrictions.
▸ BSEC fines FA Trading for inflating Star Adhesives share price
THE BUSINESS STANDARD
BSEC fined FA Trading Corporation Tk1 lakh for manipulating Star Adhesives' share price through coordinated "series trading" that drove a 43% rally, leaving the firm an unrealised gain of about Tk1.2cr.
■ 06 / SECTOR NEWS
 
▸ LNG terminal glitch cuts gas supply by 450 mmcfd nationwide
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  FUELPOWER
A technical fault at the Moheshkhali floating LNG terminal cut national gas supply by roughly 450mmcfd, triggering CNG queues and industrial disruption nationwide; Petrobangla says repairs are underway with no firm timeline.
▸ PM assures businesses of improving energy supply within a year
THE BUSINESS STANDARD  TEXTILE
PM Tarique Rahman told BGMEA and BTMA leaders the gas and power crisis will visibly ease within one to one-and-a-half years, with a decision on a new floating LNG terminal due within a month; industry groups warn fresh investment will stall until supply stabilises.
